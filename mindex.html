Core full-year module:

- 6CCS3PRJ - Individual Project - 30 credits

Credit Value : 30
Coordinator: Dr Natalia Criado-Pacheco
Semester: 1 & 2
Assessment structure: 100% final report
Description: The project provides you with the oportunity to engage in a self-managed and detailed investigation of an aspect of computer science. You will develop skills in relation to: literature searching, retrieval and analysis , report writing, the oral presentation of material , project planning and management.

Term 1:

Compulsory:

- 6SSMN325 Business Strategy - 15 credits

Credit Value : 15
Coordinator: Dr Sarah Park, Dr Janja Tardios
Semester: 1
Assessment structure: 81% written examination, 10% group presentation, 9% tutorial attendance and participation
Description: The central focus of our class is on business and corporate strategic management as opposed to functional strategies discussed in functional courses (like marketing or HRM). The emphasis will be on how the two levels of strategy are developed, how they lead to competitive advantage and how they are related.

Optional:

- 6CCS3PAL Parallel Algorithms - 15 Credits  

Credit Value: 15
Coordinator: Professor Colin Cooper
Semester: 1
Assessment structure: 100% written examination
Description: On successful completion of this module you will understand the creation of efficient parallel algorithms in a range of application areas, including sorting, matrix and graph based problems.

- 6CCS3CIS Cryptography and Information Security - 15 Credits  

Credit Value: 15
Coordinator: Professor Luca Vigano
Semester: 1
Assessment structure: 100% written examination
Description: On successful completion of this module you should be able to understand the relevant mathematical techniques associated with cryptography; understand the principles of cryptographic techniques and perform implementations of selected algorithms in this area; appreciate the application of security techniques in solving real-life security problems in practical systems.

- 6CCS3CFL Compilers and Formal Languages - 15 Credits  

Credit Value: 15
Coordinator: Dr Christian Urban
Semester: 1
Assessment structure: 80% written examination, 20% coursework
Description: At the end of this module students will have learned how to use regular expressions to scrape information from the web, how to design grammars for parsing languages and how to implement a small interpreter and compiler. Students will be able to implement the central components of a small compiler. Students will also know the theory behind lexing and parsing so that they canchoose an appropriate algorithm for recognising a computer language.

- 6CCS3AMS Agents and Multi-Agent Systems - 15 Credits  

Credit Value: 15
Coordinator(s): Dr Elizabeth Black, Professor Michael Luck
Semester: 1
Assessment structure: 80% written examination, 20% coursework
Description: This module provides a critical introduction to theories and methods regarding multi-agent computer systems and their component agents, focussing particularly on mechanisms for coordination and agreement between self-interested agents.

- 6CCS3COV Computer Vision - 15 Credits  

Credit Value: 15
Coordinator: Dr Michael Spratling
Semester: 1
Assessment structure: 65% written examination, 35% coursework
Description: Computer Vision is concerned with the automatic interpretation and analysis of images. This module aims to provide a comprehensive introduction to the subject, covering the basic concepts, methodologies and tools of image analysis and interpretation. This module will also explore the biological visual system, which has solved the problem of vision far better than contemporary computer vision systems. This will highlight the limitations of computer vision and suggest approaches for building improved, biologically-inspired, vision systems.

- 6CCS3CSL Computer Science Logic - 15 Credits  

Credit Value: 15
Coordinator: Dr Hana Chockler
Semester: 1
Assessment structure: 100% written examination
Description: This module intends to show the importance of formal, logical modelling in the development of hardware and software systems. The module introduces variouslogical formalisms and shows how they can be used as specification, design andvertification tools in Computer Science and IT. The module presents alsoformalisms for knowledge representation and reasoning used in AI systems.

- 6CCS3HCI Human-Computer Interaction - 15 Credits  

Credit Value: 15
Coordinator: Dr Rita Borgo
Semester: 1
Assessment structure: 85% written examination, 15% coursework
Description: The aim of this module is to introduce essential knowledge and methods for developing computer systems that are usable, useful and enjoyable - with a particular focus on the user experience. The module covers advanced topics and techniques for understanding user needs and requirements; designing systems; and evaluating their usability.

- 6CCS3VER Formal Verification - 15 Credits  

Credit Value: 15
Coordinator: Dr Hana Chockler, Professor Luca Vigano, Dr Daniele Magazzeni
Semester: 1
Assessment structure: 75% written examination, 25% coursework
Description: This module covers the state-of-the art in Model Checking and Formal Verification, looking at formal models to represent systems, protocols, etc., and different formal techniques and tools for security verification and  model checking.

- 6CCS3WSN Algorithms for the World Wide Web and Social Networks - 15 Credits  

Credit Value: 15
Coordinator: Professor Colin Cooper
Semester: 1
Assessment structure: 100% written examination
Description: This module aims to provide students with a general introduction to algorithms for large online networks with a main focus on the www and social networks.

- 6CCS3SMT Software Measurement and Testing - 15 Credits  

Credit Value: 15
Coordinator: Dr Hanna Chockler, Dr Jose Such
Semester: 1
Assessment structure: 100% written examination
Description: This module aims to convey an understanding of the basic elements of software design, development, and testing cycle. By the end, students should be able to describe, apply and critique a number of well known software metrics for testing adequacy.  They should also be able to describe and apply several techniques for checking software correctness that go beyond testing. 

- 6CCS3GRS Computer Graphics Systems - 15 Credits  

Credit Value: 15
Coordinator: Dr Richard Overill
Semester: 1
Assessment structure: 100% written examination
Description: This module aims to introduce you to the computational, mathematical and physical principles underlying computer graphics systems. Upon following this module successfully you will be able to apply the computational, mathematical and physical principles that are introduced to create realistic 2D, 3D Wireframe and 3D rendered images and animations.

- 6CCS3AIN Artificial Intelligence - 15 Credits  

Credit Value: 15
Coordinator: Professor Simon Parsons, Dr Elizabeth Black
Semester: 1
Assessment structure: 80% written examination, 20% coursework
Description: This module to provide a grounding in artificial intelligence techniques used to represent knowledge about the world, to make decisions on the basis of that information, and to update what is known about the world.

- 6CCS3AIP Artificial Intelligence Planning - 15 Credits  

Credit Value: 15
Coordinator: Dr Amanda Coles, Dr Daniele Magazzeni
Semester: 1
Assessment structure: 75% written examination, 25% coursework
Description: This module covers the state-of-the-art in Artificial Intelligence planning, looking at the theoretical details behind planning; the implementation of planning techniques; and how planning can be used effectively in application.

Term 2:

Compulsory:

- 6SSMN339 Human Resource Management - 15 credits

Credit Value : 15
Coordinator: Ricardo Rodrigues
Semester: 2
Assessment structure: 70% individual essay, 20% group presentation, 10% tutorial assessment
Description: You will be introduced to the contemporary debates, theories and concepts which underpin people management. Emphasis is placed on understanding how HRM as a function relates to strategic business decision-making and its implications in the globalised workplace. It also demonstrates the importance people management plays in achieving competitive advantage in an increasingly complex and globalised business environment.

Optional:

- 6CCS3OME Optimisation Methods - 15 Credits  

Credit Value: 15
Coordinator: Dr Kathleen Steinhoefel
Semester: 2
Assessment structure: 100% written examination
Description: On successful completion of this module you will be able to express computational problems from various application areas as (discrete) optimisation problems; will be familiar with commonly used algorithms and main algorithmic techniques for optimisation problems; will be aware of the principles underpinning the discussed algorithms, will be able to select an appropriate algorithm or a general algorithmic technique for a given optimisation problem; will be familiar with the running time of the discussed algorithms.

- 6CCS3DSM Distributed Systems - 15 Credits  

Credit Value: 15
Coordinator: Dr Simon Miles
Semester: 2
Assessment structure: 100% written examination
Description: This module aims to provide an overall understanding of the basic concepts and practical technology of distributed computing. It also aims to provide an in-depth understanding of the considerations applied in designing software for distributed systems. The module will give students knowledge of algorithmic and architectural techniques used to address these considerations, and provide means of assessing how well novel technologies are able to handle faults in distributed systems.

- 6CCS3COM Computational Models - 15 Credits  

Credit Value: 15
Coordinator: Professor Maribel Fernandez
Semester: 2
Assessment structure: 100% written examination
Description: The aim of this module is to describe formal languages and the main abstract models of computation, precisely and in enough detail to distinguish their essential aspects clearly.

- 6CCS3SAD Software Architecture and Design - 15 Credits  

Credit Value: 15
Coordinator: Dr Hamza Hamza
Semester: 2
Assessment structure: 100% written examination
Description: This module aims to explore the practice of software architecting as applied to the development of enterprise software systems. To learn about software architecture, architecture patterns, frameworks, design patterns, pattern languages, layers of change, the architecting process and the practical process of software design and implementation. Ideas are put into practical perspective through an introduction to the UML2 superstructure and enterprise component middleware. By the end of this module, the student should have a broad knowledge of the issues, techniques and processes involved in architecture design, and the ability to design medium to large size software systems.

- 6CCS3SIA Software Engineering of Internet Applications - 15 Credits  

Credit Value: 15
Coordinator: Professor Stefan Edelkamp
Semester: 2
Assessment structure: 100% written examination
Description: This module will cover different approaches for building internet applications and the choice of design techniques and technologies involved from a software engineering perspective, considering issues of efficiency, modularity and maintainability. You will gain the ability to design and implement internet-based systems in a modular manner, using appropriate languages and techniques.

- 6CCS3NSE Network Security - 15 Credits  

Credit Value: 15
Coordinator: Professor Luca Vigano, Dr Jose Such
Semester: 2
Assessment structure: 100% written examination
Description: This module introduces the underlying concepts and practical applications of information security engineering at a level that reflects the current state-of-the-art. Successful completion of this course will provide students with a detailed understanding of how different types of attacks on computer systems and networks are carried out and also how such attacks can be reliably prevented or detected.

- 6CCS3OME Optimisation Methods - 15 Credits  

Credit Value: 15
Coordinator: Dr Kathleen Steinhoefel
Semester: 2
Assessment structure: 100% written examination
Description: This module introduces various discrete optimisation problems, efficient algorithms for solving these problems, and general algorithmic techniques which can be applied to a wide range of optimisation problems. The emphasis is put on network optimisation problems and on general optimisation techniques. On successful completion of this module you will be able to express computational problems from various application areas as (discrete) optimisation problems, and be familiar with commonly used algorithms and main algorithmic techniques for optimisation problems.

- 6CCS3PRE Pattern Recognition - 15 Credits  

Credit Value: 15
Coordinator: Dr Michael Spratling, Dr Hak-Keung Lam
Semester: 2
Assessment structure: 65% written examination, 35% coursework
Description: This module introduces both statistical and neural network theory and approaches for solving pattern recognition problems. By the end of the module, you should be aware of different methods, and be able to describe their main features and should have some awareness of the relative merits and appropriate applications for each method, and be able to apply these methods competently to well-defined problems in standard contexts.

- 6CCS3TSP Text Searching and Processing - 15 Credits  

Credit Value: 15
Coordinator: Professor Costas Iliopoulos, Dr Solon Pissis
Semester: 2
Assessment structure: 100% written examination
Description: This unit is devoted to algorithms processing strings and texts efficiently. These types of algorithms are used for software design in the domains of operating systems utilities, search engines on the Internet, data retrieval systems, analysis of genetic sequences, and natural language processing, for example. On completing the module you will have the basic tools to design exact and efficient algorithms for matching patterns in textual data and building indexes on files.
